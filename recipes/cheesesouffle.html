Light and Airy Cheese Soufflé

A successful cheese soufflé is light, airy, and delicately savory. The

whipped, folded-in egg whites give the soufflé its unique, fluffy texture.

Serve cheese soufflé with roasted meat like beef, ham, or lamb, and a

crisp green salad or vegetable on the side.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ tablespoons unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing ramekins
  • 2 tablespoons finely grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 ½ tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs, separated
  • ½ teaspoon ground mustard
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon white pepper
  • ¼ to ⅓ cup grated cheese (any type)
  • ¼ teaspoon cream of tartar

Steps:

  1. Gather the ingredients.

  2. Grease 2 (1-cup) soufflé dishes/ramekins with butter and

    dust with Parmesan cheese.

  3. Melt the butter over medium-low heat, then add flour and

    cook for 3 minutes, stirring constantly.

  4. In the meantime, heat the milk in the microwave at medium

    power for 30 seconds just to warm.

  5. Whisk warmed milk into the flour and butter mixture and

    continue to cook, stirring, until thickened.

  6. Whisk together the egg yolks, ground mustard, salt, and

    white pepper, then add to sauce mixture, whisking quickly to

    avoid scrambling the eggs.

  7. Stir in the grated cheese, melt and mix thoroughly.

  8. Remove from heat and allow it to cool to room temperature,

    about 15 minutes. While the custard is cooling, preheat the oven to 425 F.

  9. Beat the egg whites and cream of tartar to medium-stiff peaks using a hand

    mixer or standing mixer.

  10. Carefully and delicately fold this mixture back into the

    remaining whites until just combined.

  11. Divide between the prepared soufflé dishes, place in the

    oven, reduce the heat to 375 F, and bake 30 to 35 minutes

    depending on how well done you like your soufflés. A

    toothpick inserted in the middle should come out mostly

    clean, with no wet batter attached. Don't open the oven

    within the first 20 minutes or the soufflés will deflate.

  12. Once medium-stiff peaks form, fold 1/3 of the whites into the

    room-temperature sauce mixture.

  13. Serve the soufflé immediately while hot. Enjoy.
